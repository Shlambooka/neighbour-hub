Code of Conduct

1. Propose, Don't Impose

Got a great idea? Awesome. Bring it to the group, explain it, and listen. The goal isn't just getting it done; it's getting it done together. Share your vision, then invite others to help shape it.

2. Listen First, Speak Next

When someone shares an idea or a concern, let them finish. Breathe. Let their words land. You don't have to agree, but everyone deserves to feel heard. The best decisions usually come after the second or third person has spoken.

3. Meet Conflict with Curiosity

Disagreements will happen—it's natural when people care. When they do, try to stay curious instead of certain. Speak about how you feel, not what they did wrong. And remember: you'll likely be sitting on the same bench tomorrow. That matters more than being right.

4. Harmful Words Have No Home Here

This space is for everyone. If you bring racism, homophobia, or any kind of hate, you're choosing to step outside the circle. You don't have to be perfect, but you do have to be willing to listen and grow. The commons belongs to those who help it feel safe for all.

5. Rest Here, Too

You don't have to be productive every time you show up. The commons is also for sitting, breathing, watching the clouds, saying hello and nothing more. Your quiet presence matters. It makes the space feel safe and alive, even when you're not "doing" anything.
